Abstract:

We revise Krein's extension theory of semi-bounded Hermitian operators by reducing the problem to finding all positive and contractive extensions of the "resolvent operator" \((I + T)^{-1}\) of \(T\). Our treatment is simpler and more natural than Krein's original method based on the Krein transform \((I - T)(I + T)^{-1}\). We impose no constraints on \(T\) beyond positivity and symmetry - neither closedness nor domain density requirements. Furthermore, our results hold equally in both real and complex Hilbert spaces.
